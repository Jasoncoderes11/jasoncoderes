What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or intellectual property without proper acknowledgment or permission, presenting it as your own. It is considered unethical and can lead to severe consequences in academic, professional, and creative fields.

Discover More

Types of Plagiarism:

Direct Plagiarism: Copying text or ideas word-for-word without citation.

Reusing your previous work (e.g., in different classes or publications) without proper disclosure.

Paraphrasing Plagiarism: Rewriting someone else's ideas or text in your own words without crediting the source.

Mosaic Plagiarism: Mixing phrases, ideas, or sentences from various sources without proper attribution.

Accidental Plagiarism: Failing to cite sources correctly or misunderstanding citation rules..

Discover More

Consequences of Plagiarism:

Academic penalties (failing grades, suspension, or expulsion)

Legal consequences (copyright infringement lawsuits)

Damage to reputation and credibility

Loss of professional opportunities

Discover More

Tips to Avoid Plagiarism:

Understand Citation Rules: Familiarize yourself with the required citation style (e.g., APA, MLA, Chicago).

Use Quotation Marks: Enclose directly quoted material in quotation marks and provide proper attribution.

Paraphrase Correctly: Ensure paraphrased content is significantly different in structure and wording, and cite the source.

Use Plagiarism Detection Tools: Tools like Turnitin, Grammarly, or Copyscape can help verify originality.

Keep Track of Sources: Maintain a detailed record of references for your work.

What is Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software program (malware) designed to spread between devices and disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems. It requires a host program or file to execute and replicate itself, often spreading when the infected file or program is shared or executed

What is Malware Spam?

Malware spam (also known as malspam) is a type of malicious email spam used by cybercriminals to distribute malware. These emails often appear legitimate, using tactics like impersonating reputable companies, services, or individuals, to trick recipients into taking actions that lead to the installation of malicious software on their devices.

What is AntiVirus?
